Warning 4.5. Avoid Losing Solutions.

Be cautious when performing algebraic operations as the domain of the expressions involved may change resulting in a non-equivalent equation. This can lead to lost solutions or extraneous values that are not solutions to the original equation. For example, the equation
\begin{equation*} 4x^2 = 2x \end{equation*}
is not equivalent to
\begin{equation*} 2x = 1 \end{equation*}
as dividing both sides by \(2x\) assumed it was non-zero. Doing so lost a solution to the first equation of \(x=0\text{.}\)
